# Shower to Table — Fold-Together Place Cards

A bridal-shower gift that turns into your wedding reception place cards. Friends and family fold printed origami crane sheets at the shower; the couple takes them home; those same cranes hold name cards on the wedding tables.

One gift, two big days.

---

## How it works

Most shower gifts get used once and forgotten. This one gets made together at the shower and then shows up again at the wedding.

Each set ships as **flat printed sheets** — not pre-folded. The fold lines are printed on the paper so anyone can follow along. No origami experience needed. Just a table, some time, and the people who came to celebrate you.

What makes it different from ordering place cards online is **who folds them** and **when**. The shower is where they get made. The wedding is where they get used.

---

## Three steps

Every theme follows the same path.

### Step 1 — At the shower

**What happens:** Someone opens the box and passes out sheets. Guests sit around the table, follow the printed guides, and fold cranes together.

**What it feels like:** A fun group activity — more interesting than another shower game.

**What you leave with:** A pile of cranes, each one folded by someone who was there.

### Step 2 — After the party

**What happens:** The folded cranes go home with the couple in a box or bag until the wedding.

**What it feels like:** A gift that's already doing its job before the wedding day.

**What you leave with:** Place card holders with a story — not something you'll throw away.

### Step 3 — On the wedding day

**What happens:** Name cards slide into each crane and they go on the reception tables.

**What it feels like:** The shower and the wedding connected, without anyone having to make a speech about it.

**What you leave with:** Table details that guests actually notice and ask about.

## Three themes to start

Same fold, same three steps. Different colors and patterns on the paper.

### 1. Good Wishes

**Tagline:** Each guest folds a crane and tucks in a little wish.

**The story:** Based on the tradition of folding paper cranes for good luck. At the shower, everyone folds one and can write a short wish on the sheet first. By the end you've got cranes that literally contain nice things people said about you.

**Good for:** Showers where you want something sweet but low-pressure. People who hate public speaking can write a wish and fold quietly.

**At the wedding:** Soft blue and white cranes on the tables. Simple and easy to spot.

**Colors:** Sky blue, white, soft blue ink.

---

### 2. Garden Party

**Tagline:** For couples who already have flowers everywhere.

**The story:** Light green paper with small leaf and vine prints. Everyone adds another crane to the pile — a bit like potting plants together. Works well if your shower and wedding both have an outdoor, garden-y feel.

**Good for:** Outdoor showers, spring weddings, anyone buying eucalyptus who doesn't want place cards that clash with the florals.

**At the wedding:** Sage-green cranes that sit nicely next to centerpieces.

**Colors:** Sage, white, green ink, blush accent.

---

### 3. Night Sky

**Tagline:** Each guest folds a star. Together they make a constellation.

**The story:** Deep blue paper with tiny star prints. Each guest folds one crane and it gets a little star mark — so at the wedding all the cranes together look like a night sky. Nice for evening receptions or couples who like stargazing.

**Good for:** Whimsical but not over-the-top. Also works if you want to lean into "these are the people who matter most."

**At the wedding:** Navy cranes with white star details. Looks good with candles.

**Colors:** Navy, light lavender, white stars, soft yellow accent.
