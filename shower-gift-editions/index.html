How it works

A group gift you actually do together.

Each set comes as flat printed sheets — not pre-folded. The fold lines are printed right on the paper, so guests can follow along without any origami experience. All you need is a table and a little time.

What makes this different from buying place cards online is who does the folding and when. The shower is where the cranes get made. The wedding is where they get used. Same paper cranes, two parts of the same story.

Three steps

Shower → home → wedding.

Every theme follows the same path. Pick the look you like — the steps stay the same.

At the shower

Everyone folds together

Someone opens the box and passes out sheets. People sit around the table, follow the printed guides, chat, help each other with tricky folds, and slowly fill the room with little paper cranes.

Feels like A fun group activity — more interesting than another round of bingo.

After the party

The cranes go home with you

The folded cranes leave in a shoebox, tote bag, whatever works. They sit on a shelf until the wedding gets closer. You already know who's coming to the reception — you watched them make these.

Feels like A gift that's already useful before the wedding even happens.

On the wedding day

They become your place cards

Slide a name card into each crane and set them on the tables. Guests who were at the shower spot the ones they folded. Everyone else just thinks they look nice — which they do.

Feels like The shower and the wedding connected, without anyone having to explain it.

Theme 1

Good Wishes

Each guest folds a crane and tucks in a little wish.

Based on the old tradition of folding paper cranes for good luck. At the shower, everyone folds one and can write a short wish on the sheet before folding. By the end you've got a pile of cranes that literally contain nice things people said about you.

Good for

Showers where you want something sweet but low-pressure. Even people who hate public speaking can write a wish and fold quietly.

At the wedding: Soft blue and white cranes on the tables. Simple, cheerful, easy to read from across the room.

Theme 2

Garden Party

For couples who already have flowers everywhere.

Light green paper with little leaf and vine prints along the edges. Folding feels a bit like potting plants together — everyone adds another crane to the pile. Works especially well if your shower and wedding both have a garden-y vibe going on.

Good for

Outdoor showers, spring weddings, anyone who's already buying a lot of eucalyptus and doesn't want the place cards to clash.

At the wedding: Sage-green cranes that sit nicely next to floral centerpieces without fighting for attention.

Sage · white · green ink · blush accent

Theme 3

Night Sky

Each guest folds a star. Together they make a constellation.

Deep blue paper with tiny star prints. Each guest folds one crane and it gets a little star sticker or mark — so at the wedding, all the cranes together look like a night sky. Nice if you met at a late-night diner or just like stargazing.

Good for

Evening receptions, couples who want something a little whimsical without going full fairy-tale. Also works if your guest list feels like "the people who matter most" and you want that on the nose.

At the wedding: Navy cranes with white star details. Looks great with candles on the table.

Navy · lavender · white stars · soft yellow accent
